The Wii Remote

Picture of a default Wii Remote with Wii Motion Plus inside:

The Wii Remote is a massive contributor to the Wii’s success. The Wii Remote’s game changing gimmick was its motion controls. These motion control allowed for new games to be played like no other game beforehand, such as in “The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess” or “The Legend of Zelda: Skyward Sword” where you have the ability to swing the Wii Remote as if it was the protagonists sword causing the protagonist to swing along with your motion. The uniqueness of mechanics such as this contributed to the Wii’s skyrocket in popularity. The Wii Remote had many different attachments to go along with it such as the Nunchuk, which is a separate smaller controller that had 2 buttons along with a joystick. The Wii Remote’s surface consisted of: A home button, power button, D-pad, 3 other buttons, an LED display to show controllers battery level / player number, and a speaker. The Wii Remote’s attachments range from useful ones such as the Wii MotionPlus which increased accuracy of how the Wii picked up movement, to far less useful ones such as the Wii Zapper which was just a gun. The Wii Remote was customizeable as well, with numerous blank colors there was also limited edition controllers being sold such as gold colored controllers with the Triforce from “The Legend of Zelda” branded on it. The Wii Remote sold for around $50 USD buying one separately from the Wii console. Overall, the Wii would not have been near as successful as it was without the Wii Remote.
